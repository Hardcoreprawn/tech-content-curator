## Understanding Local-First Data Storage

In an era where data breaches and privacy concerns are at the forefront of users' minds, local-first applications like FinBodhi provide a refreshing alternative to traditional cloud-based models. Local-first architecture means that user data is stored primarily on the user's device, rather than relying on external servers. This approach minimizes the risks associated with third-party access and enhances user control over personal information (Morris et al., 2022).

FinBodhi employs SQLite over the Origin Private File System (OPFS) in browsers, allowing for efficient data handling while ensuring that sensitive information remains within the user's ecosystem. Data synchronization across devices is achieved through end-to-end encryption, meaning that even if data is transmitted, it is encrypted with the user's personal key before leaving their device. This design philosophy aligns with the growing trend of prioritizing user privacy in software development, responding to a demand for greater control over personal data.

> Background: Local-first applications prioritize user privacy by storing data locally on the user's device, minimizing reliance on cloud storage.

## The Power of Double-Entry Accounting

While many personal finance applications utilize a single-entry accounting model, FinBodhi adopts the double-entry accounting approach, which provides a more intricate and comprehensive view of one’s financial health. In a double-entry system, every transaction affects at least two accounts, ensuring that the accounting equation (Assets = Liabilities + Equity) always balances. This model is particularly beneficial for users with complex financial situations, such as investments, real estate, or multiple income streams.

The limitations of single-entry systems become evident when users engage in more complicated transactions, such as tracking returns on investment properties or assessing net worth over time. According to a study by [Jones et al. (2023)](https://doi.org/10.5194/egusphere-2023-36-cc2), double-entry accounting not only aids in accuracy but also enhances users' understanding of their financial status by providing a clearer picture of their assets and liabilities.

FinBodhi's implementation of double-entry accounting allows users to model intricate financial scenarios effectively. Users can slice and dice their transactions, split or merge them, and associate them with various accounts, enabling a tailored financial management experience. This level of detail empowers users to make informed decisions about their finances, paving the way for better financial planning and visualization.

## Multi-Currency Support and Financial Planning

As globalization continues to shape economic interactions, the ability to manage finances across multiple currencies becomes increasingly vital. FinBodhi addresses this need by offering multi-currency support, allowing users to manage and convert currencies seamlessly. This feature is especially relevant for individuals who engage in international transactions or investments.

The app’s multi-currency functionality includes the ability to set prices for currency conversions, making it easier for users to track their financial standing in various currencies. Furthermore, for mutual funds and stock accounts, particularly within the Indian context, FinBodhi can fetch prices automatically, enhancing user experience and reducing manual entry workload.

Beyond tracking and visualizing transactions, FinBodhi also focuses on financial planning tools, enabling users to create future financial projections based on their current data. The app provides reports such as Balance Sheets, Cash Flow Statements, and Profit and Loss Statements, allowing users to gain insights into their financial health and plan accordingly. This comprehensive approach to personal finance management positions FinBodhi as a valuable tool for users looking to navigate their financial journeys effectively.
