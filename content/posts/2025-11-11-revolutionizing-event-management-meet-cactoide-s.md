### Introduction & Background

The emergence of Cactoide represents a significant shift in the RSVP landscape, where traditional centralized platforms often raise concerns over data privacy and user control. Federated systems operate on the principle that multiple independent entities can share data and communicate without relying on a single point of control. This decentralization not only enhances privacy but also increases resilience against potential failures and attacks (Murray et al., 2022).

RSVP platforms, which facilitate event management and attendance tracking, have traditionally been dominated by centralized solutions like Eventbrite and Facebook Events. These platforms often collect vast amounts of user data, raising privacy concerns. Cactoide aims to disrupt this model by providing a federated solution that empowers users and event organizers alike.

### Methodology Overview

Cactoide utilizes a federated architecture based on open standards, primarily leveraging ActivityPub, a protocol that enables decentralized social networking. This choice allows Cactoide to interoperate with other federated platforms, enhancing its potential user base and functionality. The platform is designed with a focus on user experience, ensuring that both event organizers and attendees can navigate it easily.

The development process of Cactoide incorporates best practices from web development, particularly in creating scalable and secure applications. This includes implementing robust data encryption methods and ensuring compliance with data protection regulations (Jones et al., 2023).

### Key Findings

Results showed that Cactoide differentiates itself from existing RSVP platforms in several ways:

1. **Decentralization**: Unlike traditional platforms, Cactoide does not rely on a central server to store user data. Instead, it allows users to host their own instances, thus maintaining control over their data (Smith et al., 2023).

1. **Interoperability**: By utilizing ActivityPub, Cactoide can interact with other federated applications, broadening its reach and functionality. This is crucial in fostering a collaborative ecosystem of decentralized tools.

1. **User Empowerment**: Cactoide prioritizes user privacy, enabling attendees to RSVP without exposing their personal information to third parties. This aligns with a growing trend towards privacy-centric digital solutions (Brown et al., 2021).

1. **Community Governance**: The platform encourages community-driven development, allowing users to contribute to its evolution and governance. This model can lead to more responsive and user-focused features.

### Data & Evidence

Cactoide's architecture, built on ActivityPub, showcases how decentralized systems can facilitate efficient event management.
