## Understanding the gTLD Landscape

### What is a gTLD?

A generic Top-Level Domain (gTLD) is the last segment of a domain name, following the final dot, such as .com, .org, or .meow. gTLDs are vital for the structure of the Domain Name System (DNS), allowing users to create unique web addresses. The introduction of new gTLDs has revolutionized the way individuals and organizations can brand themselves online. As of 2023, there are thousands of gTLDs available, providing opportunities for niche markets and personalized web identities (ICANN, 2023).

> Background: The Domain Name System (DNS) is a hierarchical naming system that translates human-readable domain names into IP addresses, enabling web browsers to load internet resources.

### The Role of ICANN

The Internet Corporation for Assigned Names and Numbers (ICANN) plays a crucial role in the management of gTLDs. Established in 1998, ICANN oversees the allocation of domain names and IP addresses, ensuring that the internet remains stable and secure. The ICANN Application Support Program is particularly notable for its commitment to inclusivity, providing financial assistance to applicants from underrepresented communities (ICANN, 2023). This initiative aligns perfectly with the ethos of the .meow project, which seeks to empower queer individuals and communities.
